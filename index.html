Project Outline

In the United States, the economy was the #1 vote issue in the 2024 Presidential Election. This project will analyze and compare common economic and cost of living metrics provided by the federal government to determine if these metrics are accurate and representative of the economy as a whole. These metrics are:

  • Inflation Rate
  • Inflation Rate (derived directly from consumer goods)
  • Household Income
  • Housing Prices

Which will be taken from the USDA and the FRED.

Many people complain about inflation and the cost of living rising in the United States at an unmanageable rate, which would explain why the economy is so important to the average voter. However, are complaints about inflation valid? Is inflation higher than it seems or is officially listed? Hopefully, we can address these questions and find out why Americans think the economy is such an important issue.

Data used:
Fruit and Vegetable prices: USDA
Inflation,Housing,Income: FRED

The data from the USDA is structured as:

  • Fruit/Vegetable/etc: The kind of food
  • Form: The form it is sold in (can, fresh, etc)
  • RetailPrice: The retail price of the item, on average, for the given year.
  • RetailPriceUnit: The unit the price is being measured in. So if RetailPrice is 2.00 and RetailPriceUnit is per pound, the product is being sold for 2 dollars a pound
  • Yield: Is an approximate measure of what percentage of the product is actually usable, so only about 90 percent of an apple is edible food.
  • CupEquivalentSize: The size of the item converted to cups
  • CupEquivalentUnit: The unit used in the conversion(usually the same as or equivalent to RetailPriceUnit)
  • CupEquivalentPrice: The standardized cost of the item in cups

Analysis

As you can see, the linear regression model shows that Income is not correlated with Inflation or Housing prices. It would conclude that a significant portion of the comlpaints or worries the American people have about the econonomy are either directly or indirectly related to this fact: Wages are not keeping up. This is of serious concern to anybody invested in the future of America for a number of reasons. Inflation isn't the real problem here, and despite this is often pointed towards as an indicator of economic stability when, by itself, really doesn't say much. If the US, for whatever reason, got rid of cents and changed the lowest denomination to be 1 dollar and everyone multiplied the price of everything by 100 to account for this, inflation would skyrocket to what would appear to be a disasterous level while in reality nothing has substantively changed.

Conclusions

Economic metrics must always be considered with the context from which they are taken. Inflation by itself means nothing just like salary by itself means nothing. If I tell you the average salary in Japan is 6,200,000 million Yen, that means nothing if you don't also know how much anything actually costs in the country, like education, housing, healthcare, and so on. For America, the inflation by itself isn't a problem, not right now. I posit that stagnating wages, or rather the median household income, are not keeping up with the prices of basic consumer goods or other expensive big ticket items such as housing.
